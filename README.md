# EU Real Estate Market Analysis (2020–2024)

## Project Overview

This project provides a comprehensive analysis of real estate market trends across Europe, focusing on property pricing, market activity, property characteristics, and investment potential.

The objective is to deliver actionable insights for real estate analysts, property investors, and marketplace product teams to better understand market dynamics, identify pricing drivers, and evaluate high-value investment opportunities.

---

## Dataset Description

The dataset consists of 5,000 property listings across multiple European countries, including:

* Property details (type, size, bedrooms, year built)
* Financial metrics (sale price, rental price, price per square meter)
* Market indicators (days on market)
* Location data (city, country)
* Property features (amenities, furnishing, energy rating)

---

## Tools Used

* Power BI Desktop
* Power Query (Data Cleaning & Transformation)
* DAX (Data Modeling & Calculations)
* GitHub (Version Control & Documentation)

---

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

* **Total Listings:** 5,000 (**+24.8% YoY**)
* **Total Sales Revenue:** €3.11 Billion (**+25.4% YoY**)
* **Average Sale Price:** €1.05 Million (**+0.1% YoY**)
* **Average Price per sqm:** €3,150 (**+0.3% YoY**)
* **Average Rent:** €3,660 (**-0.6% YoY**)
* **Average Days on Market:** 163 days (**-6.7% YoY**)
* **Total Rental Value:** €7.49 Million (**+23.4% YoY**)
* **Average Property Size:** 183.49 sqm

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Strong Market Expansion with Stable Pricing

The total number of listings increased by **24.8%**, while total sales revenue grew by **25.4%**, indicating strong market expansion.

However, average sale price and price per square meter remained relatively stable, suggesting that growth is primarily driven by increased listing volume rather than price inflation.

---

### 2. Market Activity is Improving

The average days on market declined by **6.7%**, indicating improved market liquidity and faster transaction cycles.

This suggests increasing demand and improved efficiency in property transactions.

---

### 3. Apartments Dominate Supply

Apartments account for the largest share of listings (**2,057 properties**), followed by villas and offices.

This highlights strong demand and supply concentration in residential apartment markets.

---

### 4. Property Type Influences Market Dynamics

Retail and mixed-use properties exhibit the **highest days on market (170 and 166 days)**, indicating slower sales cycles.

In contrast, villas have the **lowest days on market (155 days)**, suggesting stronger demand or faster transaction processes.

---

### 5. Balanced Market Between Sales and Rentals

* Sales: **59.1%**
* Rentals: **40.9%**

This indicates a well-balanced market with both ownership and rental opportunities.

---

### 6. Rental Market Shows Mixed Signals

While total rental value increased significantly (**+23.4%**), average rent slightly declined (**-0.6%**), suggesting increased supply or pricing competition in the rental market.

---

### 7. Country-Level Market Segmentation

Four distinct market patterns emerge:

* **High Price & Slow Market:** Netherlands
* **High Price & Fast Market:** France
* **Low Price & Slow Market:** Poland, Portugal, Spain, Belgium
* **Low Price & Fast Market:** Austria, Italy, Czech Republic, Germany

This segmentation provides a clear framework for understanding regional market behavior.

---

### 8. Pricing Trends Over Time

* Rental prices peaked in **2022 (€4,257)** before stabilizing
* Sale prices dropped in 2022 (€0.96M) before recovering in later years

This suggests temporary market correction followed by stabilization.

---

### 9. Older Properties Dominate Market Value

Properties built between **1980 and 1989** generated the highest total sales value, indicating strong demand for mid-aged properties.

Newer properties (post-2019) contribute significantly less to total sales value, likely due to limited supply.

---

### 10. Investment Opportunities Identified

Markets with:

* Lower prices
* Faster turnover

(e.g., Germany, Austria) present attractive entry points for investors.

Meanwhile, high-price markets like France offer premium value but may require higher capital.

---

## Strategic Recommendations

* Focus investment on **low-price, fast-moving markets** for higher turnover potential
* Target **apartment segment** due to high demand concentration
* Optimize pricing strategies in **slow-moving markets** (Retail, Mixed-use)
* Monitor rental supply growth to manage pricing pressure
* Explore mid-aged properties (1980s–1990s) as high-performing assets

---

## Conclusion

The European real estate market demonstrates strong growth driven by increased listings and improved transaction efficiency. While pricing remains stable, significant variations exist across countries, property types, and market segments.

Understanding these dynamics enables better decision-making and highlights clear opportunities for strategic investment.
